Description

In the book of Jeremiah (and additionally further parts of the OT), are the nations ever the object of God’s compassion (“שׁוב”)? Explicitly or implicitly? o Within the immediate context, it would seem odd that God’s compassion upon “my wicked neighbors, who touch the inheritance of my people Israel” is highlighted, rather than the compassion upon his people. So, is God’s compassion upon the nations highlighted elsewhere?

-> Since no results show up for the nations as the object of "שׁוב" or "רחם", the query has to be modified to look for examples of nations mentioned in Jeremiah (Babylon, Edom, Egypt, etc). -> Further, to include (some possible) implicite references, the query has been modified to include cases of: Predicate "שׁוב" or "רחם" o + 3mp pronominal suffix + "nations" (or concrete exmple for that matter) in a clause close by -> Final modification is to generally look at who the object of God's compassion is
